# Downsizing or Closing Your Facility?
Avoid costly mistakes when downsizing your office‚Äîsecurely recycle, resell, and save time with expert help.
![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68070a86a7562a602a623675/69063647e5cd9e61a90913d3_business_people_carrying_office_boxes_indoors_main.avif)
Thanks to Covid-19, many companies have figured out that having employees work from home has more advantages than disadvantages so shedding the unwanted, unneeded overhead is a natural result.
Chances are if you are reading this, it‚Äôs crunch time or you are trying to avoid it. There are two mistakes we all want to avoid:
### Mistake #1- Throwing away the wrong stuff or too much stuff
The more you throw away, the more it costs. The more you throw away, the worse for the environment. If you throw away the wrong stuff (confidential data, e-waste, batteries, etc.) there could be negative consequences.
**Solution- Have all data storage destroyed, sell as much usable stuff as possible, recycle what is left.**  
Shred the paper, destroy digital data, recycle the bad stuff, take no chances. If you have the time, you can often sell office furniture in good condition on Craigslist or Offer Up. Most furniture and equipment are recyclable, so in the end, very little should go as trash.
### Mistake #2- Spending too much time and money
You don‚Äôt want to spend any more time or money than you absolutely have to. Your project could require a waste hauler, a shredding company, an e-waste company, a scrap metals recycler, laborers and someone to orchestrate the whole project. You can easily spend way more than you expected.
**Solution- Get the help you need to make it cost effective and simple**
One way or another, you will need help. The question is, how do you make sure you have the right help? Here‚Äôs a checklist to assist in choosing:
  * Do they have staff trained in waste reduction and recycling
  * Can they shred and destroy your paper, hard drives and other media
  * Can they provide the labor and the hauling of all your materials
  * Can they recycle furniture, e-waste, paper, etc.

## Why Recycle Batteries
Batteries contain metals, liquids, and other materials that are flammable, toxic, and hazardous to the environment if they are not handled and disposed of properly. These materials can include lead, zinc, nickel-cadmium, sulfuric acid, mercury, plastic, lithium, and more. By using an R2 Certified recycler like Modern Waste, you ensure that these dangerous substances do not contaminate local landfills or waterways.

## Battery Recycling Process
Each type of battery has different processes for material recovery and recycling. For example, lithium-ion tool batteries may be handled differently than lithium-ion cell phone batteries, even though they share the same chemistry. Tool batteries are typically composed of a group of smaller cells that can be tested and reused if viable; the rest are processed for material recovery.
### Sample Process
![Battery recycling process illustration](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68070a86a7562a602a623675/687733ca64a57a563139058e_Sample-Process.avif)
**Whole Lead Acid Batteries** are processed through the following steps:**‚Äç**
  1. **Plastic** components are washed, dried, and converted into plastic pellets for reuse in battery cases.
  2. **Lead** parts are melted, purified, and poured into ingots for manufacturing new batteries.
  3. **Battery Acid** is either neutralized into clean water or converted into sodium sulfate for industrial use.


This is a closed-loop process, where up to 98% of lead acid batteries are recycled into new ones.

## Process
###  **‚ÄúToday‚Äôs Waste, Tomorrow‚Äôs Resources‚Äù**  

At **Modern Waste** , sustainability isn‚Äôt a buzzword‚Äîit‚Äôs the foundation of everything we do. The phrase _‚ÄúToday‚Äôs waste, tomorrow‚Äôs resources‚Äù_ perfectly captures our approach: **minimize environmental impact** while **maximizing the value** recovered from materials.  

When we talk about sustainability, we focus on two key outcomes:
  * **Minimizing environmental harm**
  * **Maximizing material value**


###  **Levels of Sustainability**  

Not all sustainable practices are created equal. Here‚Äôs how we prioritize them:
  1. **Full Reuse (Highest Impact)**   
Reusing whole devices as they were originally manufactured is the gold standard. It avoids additional energy usage and **adds immediate value back** to your bottom line.
  2. **Component Reuse**   
Even if a device can‚Äôt be reused in full, parts can still be salvaged and repurposed‚Äî**minimizing manufacturing demands** and waste.
  3. **Recycling (Last Resort)**   
Recycling requires energy to **separate, melt down, and refine** raw materials. While still better than disposal, it **consumes resources** and creates emissions.


###  **Real Examples of Sustainable Practices**  

####  **üñ•Ô∏è Computers**  

Every computer has a useful lifespan, much of it dictated by software compatibility.
  * If a system can still run current software, we **sanitize the data** , **test the hardware** , and **return it to the market for reuse**.
  * If it can‚Äôt, we **salvage components** to reduce the need for manufacturing new parts.  



####  **üîã Alkaline Batteries**  

Alkaline batteries are **shredded and separated** using heat or chemicals. The recovered materials include:
  * Zinc and manganese concentrate
  * Steel
  * Paper and plastic  



These are then reused in **manufacturing** , **agriculture** , and other industries‚Äîreducing demand for virgin resources.
